Target: dev/adr/0027-placeholder-arg-vocabulary-and-embellish-eval.html · Verdict: PASS · Audited: 2026-05-31 17:39 · Auditor: general-purpose/opus/high

Verdict

PASS. Every baseline claim the ADR makes about the as-is code verifies against source. The positional-arg cap and its abort message, the identity default for the runtime/embellish slot, the four current constructor argument names, the five exported ptr_arg_* factories (and the absence of a standalone ptr_arg_symbol), the parse-into-seed behavior of the factory return, the substitute-then-eval order in ptr_resolve_upstream, and the multi-column consumer walker all confirmed at the cited lines. All proposed symbols (parse_positional_arg, parse_named_args, validate_session_input, embellish_eval, embellish_identity, embellish_symbol_to_string, ptr_arg_symbol, the vector= flags) are correctly absent from source — aspirational, not drift. Worked-examples sections are structurally complete (two positive, two negative, each with a one-line summary and an r-fenced block). No findings.

None.

Worked examples

Verdict: complete. Two positive (what becomes possible), two negative (rejected by design); each preceded by a one-line summary and containing a language-r code block.

  • positive 1 — a column consumer that survives both the ptr_app and naked-expression paths via embellish_symbol_to_string().
  • positive 2 — a numeric placeholder whose plain-R meaning is deliberately a string (D1 rationale).
  • negative 1 — bare multi-positional colvars(mpg, hp) stays rejected (one-positional cap, D6).
  • negative 2 — relying on the default embellish_identity() for a tidyselect consumer (the anti-pattern this ADR prevents).
